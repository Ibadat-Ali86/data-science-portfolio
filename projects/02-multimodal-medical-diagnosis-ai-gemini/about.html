Project Overview

VITAL-LINK (Visual-Audio-Text Integration for Lung Analysis and Linked Knowledge) is an innovative multimodal clinical reasoning system that leverages Google's Gemini 2.0 Flash model to correlate and analyze multiple medical data modalities simultaneously.

Unlike traditional single-modal diagnostic tools, VITAL-LINK integrates:

  • Chest X-Ray Images ‚Äî Visual radiological analysis for detecting consolidations, effusions, and masses
  • Lung Auscultation Audio ‚Äî Sound pattern recognition for crackles, wheezes, and rhonchi
  • Patient Vital Signs ‚Äî Clinical context including temperature, SpO2, heart rate, and blood pressure

Problem Statement

Medical diagnosis is inherently multimodal‚Äîphysicians correlate visual findings from imaging, auditory cues from stethoscopes, and numerical data from vital signs. However, most AI diagnostic tools analyze only a single modality, missing the critical correlations that lead to accurate diagnoses.

  • Fragmented Systems: Current tools require separate AI models for X-rays, audio, and vitals
  • Lack of Cross-Modal Reasoning: No correlation between what the X-ray shows and what the stethoscope hears
  • Expertise Shortage: Many regions lack access to experienced pulmonologists
  • Delayed Diagnoses: Waiting for specialist consultations can be critical in acute respiratory conditions

Our Solution

VITAL-LINK leverages Gemini 2.0 Flash's native multimodal capabilities to process all three data types in a single inference call, enabling true cross-modal correlation reasoning.

üí° Innovation Highlights

  • Single-Model Multimodal Processing: No need for separate models‚ÄîGemini handles image, audio, and text natively
  • Reasoning Trace: Transparent AI logic showing how each modality contributes to the diagnosis
  • Confidence Scoring: Quantified certainty to help clinicians prioritize cases
  • Differential Diagnosis: Ranked list of possible conditions with probabilities
  • Actionable Recommendations: Suggested next steps for clinical management
